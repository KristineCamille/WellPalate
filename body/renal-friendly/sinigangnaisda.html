SINIGANG NA BANGUS

Sinigang na Bangus is a Filipino comfort dish that tickles the taste buds with a perfect balance of sourness and savory flavors. Tender milkfish swims in a tamarind broth, mingling with an assortment of crisp vegetables. Aside from its delicious taste, this dish holds potential benefits for renal health. The moderate phosphorus content in bangus, coupled with the nutrient-rich vegetables, makes it a kidney-friendly option. By adjusting the salt levels in the broth, you can further customize it as a lower-sodium delight It's a warm hug for your soul and a burst of flavors for your palate!

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 small radish, sliced
  • 1 medium tomato, quartered
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 tablespoons tamarind mix or tamarind paste
  • Salt and fish sauce (patis) to taste
  • 2 cups spinach leaves

PROCEDURES:

  1. In a pot, bring water to a boil.
  2. Add radish, tomato, eggplant, and onion. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Dissolve tamarind mix or paste in a cup of water and strain to extract tamarind juice. Add to the pot.
  4. Add fish filets and let it cook until opaque.
  5. Season with salt and fish sauce to taste.
  6. Add spinach leaves and cook until wilted.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.
