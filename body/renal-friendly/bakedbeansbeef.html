BAKED BEANS BEEF

Baked beans with beef is a hearty and protein-packed dish that's both nutritious and kidney-friendly. Beans are a fantastic source of plant-based protein, offering a wholesome alternative to meat. The beef, when enjoyed in moderation, adds a savory depth to the dish without overwhelming your renal health. This balance makes the Baked Beans with Beef a kidney-friendly option, providing a hearty, satisfying meal that's gentle on the kidneys. So, dive into the savory goodness and relish a meal that's both delicious and considerate of your well-being. Happy and healthy eating!

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 cup dried navy beans, soaked overnight
  • 250g lean beef, cubed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

PROCEDURES:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C)
  2. In a large ovenproof pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Saute until fragrant.
  3. Add the cubed beef and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Drain and rinse the soaked beans, then add them to the pot.
  5. Stir in the tomato sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, and black pepper.
  6. Add enough water to cover the mixture, about 2 cups.
  7. Cover the pot and transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 2 to 2.5 hours, or until the beans are tender.
  8. Check occasionally and add more water if needed.

NUTRIENT FACTS:

Calories  250 kcal
Carbohydrates  28g
Protein  20g
Fat 7g
Sodium 150mg
Sugar 29g
