GINATAANG KALABASA AT SITAW

Ginataang Kalabasa at Sitaw is a creamy Filipino stew that celebrates the earthy sweetness of squash and the crunch of long beans. Simmered in a coconut milk broth with a hint of shrimp paste, it's a comforting dish that's perfect for any occasion. Beyond its delicious taste, this dish offers plenty of health benefits. The string beans provide dietary fiber and essential vitamins, while the squash adds a dose of antioxidants and nutrients. Coconut milk contributes healthy fats and a creamy texture. Together, these ingredients make Ginataang Sitaw at Kalabasa a nourishing option for heart health, digestion, and overall well-being. Indulge in a bowl that not only satisfies your cravings but also embraces the goodness of wholesome ingredients in every bite!

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 cups kalabasa (squash), cubed
  • 1 cup sitaw (long beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

PROCEDURES:

  1. In a pot, heat cooking oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant
  2. Add the shrimp paste and cook for a minute to release its flavor.
  3. Add the kalabasa and cook until slightly tender.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook until the squash is fully cooked.
  5. Add the sitaw and cook until both vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
