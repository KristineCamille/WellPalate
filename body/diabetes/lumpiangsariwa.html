LUMPIANG SARIWA

Lumpiang sariwa is a delightful Filipino dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of fresh spring rolls. The filling typically includes ingredients such as potatoes, carrots, and sometimes tofu or ground meats, providing a flavorful combination. Lumpiang sariwa not only caters to the unique palate of Filipinos but also offers a tasty and health-conscious experience that welcomes everyone to the table, particularly those with a focus on diabetic-friendly dietary preferences.

INGREDIENTS:

Filling Ingredients:
  • ¬º kilo ground chicken
  • 1 potato
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 sweet potato
  • Cabbage
  • Onion and garlic
  • Ubod
  • Olive oil
For sauce:
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • ¬Ω cup brown sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • ¬Ω pork cube
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tablespoon cornstarch

PROCEDURES:

  1. Saute the garlic, onion, and ground chicken for 5-8 mins.
  2. Add the chopped sweet potatoes, carrots, potatoes, cabbage, and ubod.
  3. Stir fry until the chicken is cooked and vegetables are half-cooked or soft enough to eat.
  4. For sauce, saute the garlic before combining all the ingredients. Make sure to dilute the cornstarch in water before pouring into the mixture.
  5. Let the sauce mixture simmer for 3-5 mins. Occasionally stir to achieve the thick consistency.
  6. For arrangement, place one lumpia wrapper on a plate. Place a piece of lettuce at its center, with the curly edges at the top. Spread 1/2 cup of the filling onto the lettuce and gently fold the side of the wrappers.
  7. Transfer to a clean plate and serve with sauce
