SALMON WITH GREEN BEANS

Salmon with green beans is a diabetes-friendly dish that combines the rich flavors of salmon with the nutritional benefits of green beans, making it a wholesome and balanced meal for those managing diabetes. In this dish, salmon, known for its omega-3 fatty acids and protein content, is often grilled or baked to perfection. It's paired with fresh green beans, providing fiber, vitamins, and minerals without significantly impacting blood sugar levels. The inclusion of healthy fats from the salmon, along with the fiber and nutrient-rich green beans, contributes to a well-rounded dish that supports stable blood sugar control. This dish is a tasty reminder that managing diabetes can be both delicious and nutritious. Enjoy the goodness and take a flavorful step towards your well-being!

INGREDIENTS:

  • Green beans
  • Salmon filet
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon or calamansi juice
  • Apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • Olive oil
  • Dried oregano

PROCEDURES:

  1. Season the salmon filet with salt, pepper, calamansi or lemon juice, vinegar, dried oregano, and minced garlic.
  2. Pan fry or grill the seasoned fish. Occasionally flip to achieve the perfect golden-brown salmon.
  3. Once the fish is cooked, set aside.
  4. Roast the green beans in a pan with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic for 8-10 mins.
  5. After roasting the beans, add the salmon and gently stir to combine flavors.
  6. Turn off the heat and serve it with a drizzle of calamansi or lemon juice.

NUTRIENT FACTS:

Calories  350kcal
Carbohydrates  4g
Protein  20g
Fat 28g
Sodium 190mg
Sugar 2g
