CHICKEN CAULIFLOWER RICE BOWL

Chicken cauliflower rice bowl is a diabetes-friendly dish that replaces traditional rice with cauliflower rice, offering a lower-carbohydrate alternative. In this bowl, cooked chicken is paired with cauliflower rice, along with a variety of vegetables and flavorful seasonings. The inclusion of lean protein from chicken further enhances its nutritional profile, providing a balanced and satisfying meal for individuals mindful of their carbohydrate intake. Enjoy the tasty and healthy combo of chicken cauliflower rice bowls—a perfect choice for those watching their diabetes.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2-3 pieces chicken breast
  • 2 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and ground pepper
  • 1-2 red bell peppers
  • 1 red onion
  • 3-4 garlic cloves
  • 1 whole cauliflower
  • 2-3 tomatoes
  • 3 green onion (leaves)

PROCEDURES:

  1. Season the chicken breast with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and ground pepper.
  2. Grill or pan-fry the chicken, occasionally flipping it to achieve an even, golden-brown exterior.
  3. For the rice bowl, chop the cauliflower into small pieces. Next is to saute it together with the onion, garlic, bell peppers, and rice. Add salt and pepper to taste. Mix it well for 3-5 minutes and add half of the green onions.
  4. For serving, place a cup of cauliflower rice in a bowl. Top it with the sliced grilled or fried chicken breast, tomatoes, and remaining green onions.
  5. You can optionally add your preferred dressing, ketchup or mayonnaise, and enjoy the delightful meal.

NUTRIENT FACTS:
